Lesson 3: Practicality of major scale

In this lesson, we will take the theroy we learned in the past lesson and try it on guitar.

First thing first

How are the notes positioned in guitar.

The starndard notes of the guitar is E B G D E A starting rom the lower string. And the rest follows as we know the notes.

So, like we did in the previous lesson, let's look at the A major scale.

Starting form the top we have E as the 0, F as the 1 and so on. Here, we have 5 of the top string as A.

Following the pattern of A B C# D E F# G# A

We get, 5 7 9 10 12 14 16 17

If we want it in the same position then, we can play the same notes but on differet strings.

So we can play

E String: 5 7

A String: 4 7

D String: 4 7

G String: 4 6

B String: 5 7

E String: 5 7

There you have it, an actual scale that can be parcticed and mastered. Now, not only do you know the pattern but you also know how the it is made.
