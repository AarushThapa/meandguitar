Lesson 4: Major Chord Formation

In this lesson, we will discuss about the formation of chords.

Ever Wonder how chords have such meledious sound.

Well, it all comes to understanding the major scale and breaking and using it.

As we know from our previous lessons the formation of sale, let's look at the major scale of A again.

A B C# D E F# G# A

1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8

Here 1 represents A 2 represents B and so on.

Let's look at the formation of A mojor with three notes.

Simply, take the notes 1, 3 and 5.

We have A C# and E

Now, use place only these three notes on the guitar fret.

If we look at the traditional A chord, we see the lower string is open, the 2nd 3rd and 4th string are pressed in 2nd fret. and the rest are open.

When checked, we get

E String: 0 E

A String: 0 A

D String: 2 E

G String: 2 A

B String: 2 C#

E String: 0 E

Finally, we learned how to make major chord. Try it with all the scale, you will get all the major chords.
