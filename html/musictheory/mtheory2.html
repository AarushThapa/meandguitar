Lesson 2: Major Scale formation

In this lesson, we will take what we have learned in the previous lesson and apply it in creating scales which then will be used to create chords and music.

To revise we have 12 notes.

A A# B C C# D D# E F F# G G#

Note: The distance between two notes is called interval.

For this lesson we will introduce two new terms, half step and whole step.

Half Step

Half step is the next note i.e:

The half step of A is A#, B has C and G has G# and so on.

Whole Step

Then, whole step is the second next note i.e:

The whole step of A is B, C has D E has F# as so on.

Now, inorder to create a major scale there is a pattern.

The pattern is

r w w h w w w h

where, r is the root note

h is the half step

w is the whole step

First pick the note whose major scale you want. Here in this example I will pick A note.

  1. The root note is A
  2. Then whole step is B
  3. Then the whole step of B is C#
  4. Then the half step of C# is D
  5. Then the whole step of D is E
  6. Then the whole step of E is F#
  7. Then the whole step of F# is G#
  8. And finally the half step of G# is A

We now have,

A B C# D E F# G# A

There we have it, a major scale of A.

If you are familiar with "sa, re, ga, ma, pa, dha, ni, sa" or "so, la, ti, do, re, mi, fa, so" then you just got the guitar to do this. If you try to play it anywhere in the guitar then you get the same sound.

If you understoon this, then congratulations you are ready to make a major scale of any note you like using the same pattern.
